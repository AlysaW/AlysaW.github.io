Work Term Three


Welcome to my Co-op work term report. This website is about my final work term at the Digital Kitchener Innovation Lab as a Full Stack Developer. The work term took place from Jan 2018 to Aug 2018 (8 months)

Digital Kitchener Innovation Lab

The City of Kitchener opened the Digital Kitchener Innovation Lab in September 2017, in the Kitchener Communitech building in the back alley.

The lab’s goal is to provide insights on how to make the city more efficient to make Kitchener a smart city.

The lab does this through IOT, Machine learning, and data analysis through the product Clara. The Lab is made up of three people the lab director and two developer co-ops.

Work Station

Goals

Oral Communication Problem Solving

Get more comfortable talking in front of larger groups.

The municipal innovation Lab became a very intriguing aspect to the community, and many people want to know what we were developing and testing. Therefore there were many impromptu demos in front of groups that would come to visit. Impromptu demos were a big thing to get used to, as well a fantastic opportunity to build communication skills. I worked with my co-worker to be prepared with critical aspects to point out about the product and organize the order of points. This preparation helped people understand the product better. By the end of the work term, I felt comfortable doing a presentation on the product that we were building and that audience would understand it.

Get to know Git at a more advanced level.

Collaborating on any code based project git is a huge part of the day to day work. At the start of the work term, I was at an intermediate level. To advance my git skills I took as my git issues that would come up within our team. Taking on as many git issues as possible made me research and learn how to solve more problems. Each time adding to my git documentation for later use if need. By the end, I felt confident in my git abilities, but I could not say that I’m at an advanced level just yet.
Depth and Breadth of Understanding Written Communication

Advance my backend development skills

In a team of two developers, each took on a role in the development process. My role was to manage backend code development and to take on a majority of backend tasks. Being a lead backend developer I wanted to make sure to make the best decisions that would lead to a good foundation for the product. Therefore, I made sure research architecture patterns when developing a new concept for the backend. The research helped with code reuse and less refactoring later on. What I learned by the end is no matter how much planning you do, there is always refactoring, and due to new understandings that come in to play primarily in the environment of a pilot project lab, we're trying and experimenting with new things is in the mandate.

Get better a writing technical documentation.

The project was developed by just two coops and was going to be passed off to the co-ops after us. We had to document everything so that they could continue with the project without asking us to come back and help describe how something works. We started documentation from day one with documentation Fridays. I would outline the big concept that I implemented that week with the reason why I did it a certain way, then describe how it worked and provided any diagrams that were necessary to explain the concepts better. I found if I jotted down notes throughout the week it help with creating better-detailed documentation.

Job Description

We are one of the new additions that have come with our participation in the smart city initiative. Our lab’s goal is to provide insights on how to make the city more efficient to make Kitchener a smart city. Our main project is creating a municipal machine learning and analytics platform called Clara. Clara is a cat that lives in the clock tower in Victoria Park, and she roams the city looking for insights in making the city more live, work, and playable. When askclara.ca gets launched you will be able to log on and view visualized data using open data, third party data (i.e., Open Weather Map and Waze), and sensor data (provided from the lights and other sensors from around the city) called Insight Tiles. They will consist mainly of graphs, which will be comparing many sets of data, description, to give you an idea of what the chart is all about. In the future, we hope to use machine learning to generate more insights based on the current trends. You will also be able to groups insight tiles into sets we call dashboards. With these collections of data, we hope to help you find new insights that will help the city in ways you’ve never considered before. We will also provide you with a suggestion form to curate ideas and find new ways to benefit the city.

Highlights

The most interesting aspect of my work term was developing a project from scratch in such a small team. The small team allowed me to wear many different hats then I wouldn't usually get the opportunity to in other co-op terms. I was a project manager, project designer, UX designer, frontend and backend developer. With the opportunity to do so many different roles I was able to learn many things that do not get touched on in my computer science classes. An example of new things I got to learn is how to think of a product from ground one and describe technical aspect to someone that does not have a technical background.
